Mustang

A Mustang is a wild breed of horses found in the American West; it originated from the Spanish horses introduced to North America in the 1500s. The breed does not result from selective breeding for a particular purpose by humans like most domesticated breeds, hence not being “bred for jobs.” Instead, the breeders captured and trained them to be used depending on how humans would need them after domestication. This breed was very important to people due to their resilience in the Western period.

Roles of Mustangs in the Western period:

  • Ridden by native American tribes for transportation and hunting as well as war.
  • Ridden by settlers for transportation.

The biggest value of the breed was its resilience. They adapted to withstand rough terrains, long droughts, adverse climatic conditions, and even lack of water and food sources, making them among the toughest horses. Today, Mustangs have taken a completely opposite direction compared to other western breeds. While other horse breeds are domesticated and used for various purposes, the mustangs are kept on designated lands as wild animals.

Modern uses of the Mustang horses include:

  • Adoption training of captured or relocated Mustangs.
  • Trail riding.
