Morgan Horse

The Morgan horse was the all-around horse of America in the past and proved essential for the existence and development of early settlers. As compared to other breeds that evolved for particular uses much later, Morgans were important, as they could do anything that was required of them either on a farm or around town. Their principal uses include transport, agriculture, and riding.

Morgans were tasked to:

  • Draw wagons and stagecoaches.
  • Work on the farm.
  • Travel.

It is worth noting that Morgans were powerful for their small size; this means that they could undertake heavy tasks without necessarily being big draft horses. Morgans were also docile and intelligent; hence, they were easily trained. They had energy to work tirelessly throughout the day.

Currently, the Morgan breed no longer serves its traditional purpose of undertaking difficult tasks due to the mechanization of agriculture. However, it still plays a crucial role in current equestrian activities.

Morgans have been mainly utilized for:

  • Driving.
  • Dressage and show riding.
  • Trail riding.
