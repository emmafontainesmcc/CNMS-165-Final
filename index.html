Horses of the American West

In the Western era of the US, which was in the period around the 1800s, there were several kinds of horses that did not serve only for riding purposes but for survival and work as well. There are several types of horses whose legacy is alive nowadays.

Mustangs are offspring of Spanish horses introduced to the Americas in the 1500s. Throughout years, the majority of the animals managed to escape and created herds of wild horses across the West of America.

The American Quarter Horse serves as one of the key Western horses. It was created to be fast at a short distance.

The Morgan horse is one of the first horse breeds to be created by the people in America.

This impact can be seen worldwide. For instance, such aspects as rodeo activities, rodeo horses, and cowboys originate from the Wild West period.
